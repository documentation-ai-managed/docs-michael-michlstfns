## Prerequisites

<Callout kind="info">

Before starting, ensure you have:
- A modern web browser like Chrome or Firefox
- An email address for account verification
- No prior setup required - everything runs in the browser

</Callout>

## Create Your Account

Follow these steps to register and log in to Michael / Michlstfns.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit the Dashboard" icon="monitor">
    Open your browser and navigate to `https://dashboard.example.com`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign Up" icon="user-plus">
    Click the **Sign Up** button. Enter your email, create a strong password, and complete the CAPTCHA.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify Email" icon="mail">
    Check your inbox for a verification email from Michael / Michlstfns. Click the link to activate your account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log In" icon="log-in">
    Return to the dashboard, enter your credentials, and access your new account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create Your First Documentation Project

Once logged in, set up your first documentation space.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click New Project" icon="plus">
    From the dashboard, select **New Documentation Space**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name Your Project" icon="edit-3">
    Enter a name like `My Project Docs` and choose a brand color such as `#3B82F6`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize Settings" icon="settings">
    Set your default language to English and enable MDX support.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and Open" icon="save">
    Click **Create**. Your project opens in the editor.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Navigate the Interface

Michael / Michlstfns provides an intuitive interface. Use these tabs to explore key areas.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Dashboard" icon="layout">
    
Overview of all your documentation spaces. View recent edits, analytics, and quick actions.

  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Editor" icon="edit">
    
Compose MDX pages with live preview. Supports components like `<Callout>` and `<Steps>`.

  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Files" icon="file-text">
    
Manage pages, upload images, and organize folders. Drag-and-drop supported.

  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Upload Your Initial Content

Add your first page with a sample MDX document.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create New Page" icon="file-plus">
    In the Files view, click **New Page** and name it `introduction.mdx`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Content" icon="code">
    Paste the following MDX example into the editor:
    
<CodeGroup tabs="MDX">
```mdx
---
title: Welcome
description: Your first page.
---

## Hello World

This is your **first MDX page** in Michael / Michlstfns.
```
</CodeGroup>

  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview and Publish" icon="eye">
    Switch to preview mode, then click **Publish** to make it live.
  </Step>
</Steps>
